[E1] Dynamic Earth

Dynamic Earth: types of rocks, formation, internal and external processes.

Types of Rocks

Type Formation Characteristics
Igneous Forms when magma or lava cools and solidifies. Often crystalline, hard, and may be intrusive (coarse-grained) or extrusive (fine-grained).
Sedimentary Forms from compacted sediments or organic remains. Layered, often contains fossils, and may be softer than igneous rocks.
Metamorphic Forms when existing rocks are changed by heat, pressure or chemicals. May be banded or foliated, harder, and more compact than the parent rock.

Internal Earth Processes

Process Description
Folding Compressional forces bend rock layers into folds such as anticlines and synclines.
Faulting Rocks fracture and move along fault lines, creating earthquakes and linear landforms.
Volcanism Magma rises and erupts at the surface as lava, ash and volcanic rocks.

External Processes

Process Description
Weathering Breakdown of rocks by physical, chemical and biological forces at the surface.
Erosion Removal and transport of rock material by water, wind, ice or gravity.
Mass wasting Downslope movement of rock and soil under gravity, including landslides and rockfalls.
